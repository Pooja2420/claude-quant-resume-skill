# Company JD Signals & Interview Expectations

When a user provides a JD from one of these companies, surface the relevant signals
so they can calibrate their resume AND prep appropriately.

---

## Citadel / Citadel Securities
**ATS keywords to mirror exactly:** quantitative research, systematic strategies,
alpha generation, backtesting, C++, Python, stochastic calculus, probability
**Resume signals they look for:** competitive programming, Putnam, olympiad medals,
top-tier GPA (3.8+), PhD/MS from target school, published research
**What comes after ATS:** math/logic test (Citadel's is notoriously hard), then 5+ technical rounds
**Note for resume:** show raw intellectual horsepower — competitions, research, hard math courses

---

## Jane Street
**ATS keywords:** trading, market making, options, probability, expected value,
game theory, mental math, Python, OCaml
**Resume signals:** exceptional math background, Putnam, research, top CS/math program
**What comes after ATS:** probability/math interview, then trading simulation
**Note for resume:** quantify intellectual achievements, not just work tasks.
"Ranked top 500 globally in ICPC" is worth more than a generic internship bullet.

---

## IMC Trading
**ATS keywords:** derivatives, options pricing, Greeks, market making, C++, Python,
low-latency, algorithmic trading, risk management
**Resume signals:** quant internship, strong CS fundamentals, trading knowledge
**What comes after ATS:** online test (math + coding), then technical + fit rounds
**Note for resume:** pair trading/systems experience; mention any trading competitions

---

## Two Sigma / DE Shaw
**ATS keywords:** machine learning, systematic trading, alternative data, factor models,
Python, R, statistical modeling, research
**Resume signals:** ML projects with real data, research papers, graduate degree valued
**What comes after ATS:** technical phone screen, then full loop (ML + stats + coding)
**Note for resume:** ML projects with measurable results are critical; Kaggle placements help

---

## Amazon
**ATS keywords:** Must use EXACT terms from JD. Amazon's ATS (Workday) does hard keyword match.
data science, machine learning, SQL, Python, A/B testing, statistical modeling,
business intelligence, forecasting, optimization
**Resume signals:** ownership, scale ("millions of customers", "petabytes"), impact
**What comes after ATS:** LP-based behavioral screen + technical
**Note for resume:** write bullets in STAR format implicitly; show scale and ownership.
Do NOT list "Leadership Principles" — demonstrate them through bullets.
Every bullet should show: what you did → scale → outcome.

---

## Google / DeepMind
**ATS keywords:** machine learning, algorithms, data structures, distributed systems,
Python, C++, SQL, statistical analysis, modeling, experimentation
**Resume signals:** coding ability (LeetCode-style), ML depth, publications
**What comes after ATS:** recruiter screen → 4-5 technical interviews (heavy algorithms)
**Note for resume:** Google values algorithmic problem-solving; mention competitive
programming. For quant/research roles: publications or strong research projects essential.

---

## Meta (FAIR / Central ML / Ads)
**ATS keywords:** PyTorch, deep learning, recommendation systems, NLP, computer vision,
large-scale ML, A/B testing, Python, C++, distributed training
**Resume signals:** publications, open-source ML contributions, research experience
**What comes after ATS:** coding screen → system design + ML design interviews
**Note for resume:** PyTorch experience prominently; for research roles, arXiv papers are gold.

---

## Microsoft
**ATS keywords:** machine learning, data science, Python, C#, Azure, SQL,
statistical modeling, algorithms, cloud computing
**Resume signals:** broad technical ability, team collaboration, system design
**What comes after ATS:** online assessment → technical + behavioral loop
**Note for resume:** Microsoft values breadth; show diverse tech stack if applicable.
MSFR (Microsoft Research) roles: publications strongly preferred.

---

## Netflix
**ATS keywords:** recommendation systems, A/B testing, experimentation, causal inference,
Python, Spark, SQL, personalization, machine learning, data pipelines
**Resume signals:** ML at scale, experimentation culture, data storytelling
**What comes after ATS:** data challenge / take-home → 4-5 interviews
**Note for resume:** highlight scale ("X million users", "Y% engagement lift").
Emphasis on business impact, not just technical implementation.

---

## Apple
**ATS keywords:** machine learning, CoreML, Python, C++, Swift, on-device ML,
privacy-preserving ML, signal processing, optimization, algorithms
**Resume signals:** systems depth, ML on constrained hardware, publications
**What comes after ATS:** technical screen → multiple rounds (strong culture fit emphasis)
**Note for resume:** Apple values privacy and efficiency; mention any work on model
compression, on-device inference, or federated learning.

---

## Nasdaq / Bloomberg
**ATS keywords:** financial data, market data, trading systems, risk analytics,
Python, Java, C++, SQL, FIX protocol, regulatory reporting, real-time systems
**Resume signals:** financial domain knowledge, systems reliability, data engineering
**What comes after ATS:** standard SWE loop + domain knowledge questions
**Note for resume:** highlight financial domain expertise, data pipeline work, reliability.

---

## Walmart / CVS
**Walmart ATS keywords:** supply chain optimization, demand forecasting, operations research,
Python, SQL, machine learning, inventory management, pricing analytics
**CVS ATS keywords:** health analytics, clinical data, pharmacy data, Python, SQL,
predictive modeling, cost optimization, outcomes analysis
**What comes after ATS:** more lenient than quant firms; behavioral + technical case
**Note for resume:** show business impact clearly; quantify cost savings or efficiency gains.
For CVS: healthcare/clinical domain knowledge is a plus.

## General ATS Platform Notes

| Company | ATS Platform | Key Quirk |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Amazon | Workday | Hard keyword match; exact phrase matters |
| Google | gHire (internal) | Algorithmic scoring; GPA and school weighted |
| Meta | Greenhouse | Skills parsing; GitHub links scanned |
| Microsoft | Workday | Keyword + years-of-experience parsing |
| Citadel | Lever | Human-first after basic filter; story matters |
| Jane Street | Internal | Very human-driven; résumé is a conversation starter |
| IMC | Greenhouse | Technical test is the real filter |
| Two Sigma | Greenhouse | ML keyword density matters |
| Nasdaq | iCIMS | Domain keyword heavy |
| Apple | Workday | Role-specific keyword match |
| Walmart | Workday | Skills section weight high |
| CVS | Taleo | Healthcare terminology parsing |

**Taleo tip** (CVS, some legacy companies): Taleo strips formatting aggressively.
Use the plainest possible formatting; avoid any special characters in section headers.

**Workday tip** (Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, Walmart): Workday parses date ranges strictly.
Use `Month YYYY – Month YYYY` — never `MM/YYYY` or `YYYY-MM`.
